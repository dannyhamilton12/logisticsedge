## 5 Critical Updates from Q1 2026

*These are the types of game-changing updates our subscribers received first.*

### 📋 **Update #1: S&S Declaration Changes (March 2026)**
**What Changed:** HMRC updated Safety & Security declaration requirements for containerised cargo from high-risk countries.

**Impact:** Importers from Turkey, Morocco, and Pakistan now need additional security data 24 hours before arrival.

**Subscriber Advantage:** Our readers saved 3-7 days in port delays by updating their broker instructions immediately. Non-subscribers faced Route 3 examinations and £200-500 exam fees per container.

**What You Would Have Learned:**
- Exact country list requiring enhanced S&S
- New data elements needed in declarations
- Broker communication template
- Timeline for implementation

---

### 💰 **Update #2: CBAM Registration Extension (February 2026)**
**What Changed:** UK government extended CBAM registration deadline from April to September 2026 due to technical issues with the portal.

**Impact:** Importers of cement, iron, steel, aluminium, and fertilizers got 6 extra months to prepare.

**Subscriber Advantage:** Subscribers redirected resources to other compliance priorities instead of rushing CBAM preparations. Non-subscribers wasted weeks on premature system setup.

**What You Would Have Learned:**
- New registration timeline with key dates
- Which products are affected
- Preparation checklist for the extended deadline
- Portal testing timeline

---

### 🚢 **Update #3: Felixstowe Terminal Capacity Changes (January 2026)**
**What Changed:** Felixstowe's CT8 terminal reduced import capacity by 30% due to infrastructure upgrades.

**Impact:** Container dwell time increased from 4 days to 7 days, with demurrage charges starting earlier.

**Subscriber Advantage:** Subscribers rerouted shipments to Southampton and London Gateway before congestion hit. Some saved £150-300 per container in demurrage fees.

**What You Would Have Learned:**
- Alternative port options with capacity
- Demurrage calculation changes
- Freight forwarder notifications needed
- Expected completion timeline for upgrades

---

### 📊 **Update #4: New UK-India FTA Preferential Rates (February 2026)**
**What Changed:** UK-India FTA Chapter 7 (textiles) preferential rates became effective 6 months early.

**Impact:** Duty on cotton fabrics, garments, and yarn dropped from 12% to 0-4% for qualifying Indian goods.

**Subscriber Advantage:** Fashion and textile importers immediately claimed preference on existing orders, saving 8-12% on landed costs. Estimated industry savings: £2.3M in Q1 alone.

**What You Would Have Learned:**
- Complete product code list with new rates
- Rules of Origin requirements for textiles
- Statement on Origin template
- Retroactive claim procedures

---

### 🔧 **Update #5: CDS System Maintenance Impacts (March 2026)**
**What Changed:** HMRC announced planned CDS downtime every Sunday 2-6am starting April 2026.

**Impact:** Weekend imports now require Monday declaration processing, affecting cash flow for PVA users.

**Subscriber Advantage:** Subscribers adjusted shipment schedules and informed brokers in advance. Non-subscribers faced unexpected delays and missed PVA deadlines.

**What You Would Have Learned:**
- Exact maintenance schedule through 2026
- Alternative declaration timing strategies
- Cash flow impact calculations
- Broker instruction updates needed
